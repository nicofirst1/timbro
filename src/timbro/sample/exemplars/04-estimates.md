# Why my estimates are wrong

My estimates are wrong because I estimate the coding and forget the rest. The coding is the small part. The rest is the job.

When I say a task takes a day, I mean the writing takes a day. I leave out the part where I read the existing code to understand it, the part where the test environment is broken and I fix it, the part where the first approach turns out wrong and I back it out. Those parts are not interruptions to the work. They are the work.

So now I estimate differently. I think about the last three tasks that looked like this one and how long they really took, not how long I wish they had taken. Then I say that number out loud even when it sounds too high, because it never is.

The honest estimate is uncomfortable in the meeting and comfortable in the sprint. The optimistic one is the other way around.
